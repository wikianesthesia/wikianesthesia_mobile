# Initial Steps
* Inform team. **Call for Help**
* Confirm cardiac arrest on monitors (EKG, Pleth, A-Line, Capnography) and check circuit
* Call for code cart
* Prioritize starting CPR
* Discontinue anesthetic agents, run IV fluids wide open
* 100% FiO2, maximum flows
* Identify Leader, Timer/Recorder, Chest Compression Line, Meds Administrator

# CPR/Rhythm Checks
* Rate 100-120 compressions/min
* Insert backboard when available
* Rotate compressors every 2 minutes with rhythm check
* If rhythm switches to Asystole or PEA, go to [Asystole/PEA](Basic_PEA)
* Titrate to EtCO2 > 10 mmHg and DBP > 20 mmHg
* Check pulse ONLY if signs of ROSC (sustained increased EtCO2, spontaneous arterial waveform, rhythm change)

# Defib
* Continue CPR while charging defibrillator
* Give one shock **ASAP**: 120-200J Biphasic
* Resume CPR immediately. Increase electrical energy with each shock

# Medications
* After first round of CPR, give **Epinephrine** 1mg IV every 3-5 min
* Can replace one dose of epinephrine with **Vasopresin** 40 Units IV
* If VT or hyperkalemic, consider **Calcium Chloride** 1 g IV
* Consider **Amiodarone** 300 mg IV or Lidocaine 1-1.5 mg/kg IV q3-5 min x3
* If Torsades: consider **Magnesium Sulfate** 2 g IV
* If hyperkalemia: calcium chloride 1 g IV; sodium bicarbonate 1 amp IV (50 mEq); regular insulin 5 - 10 units IV with dextrose/D50 1 amp IV (25 g)

# Additional Steps
* **Consider ECMO** promptly
* Consider focused TTE or ultrasound
* Consider [H's and T's](HandTs)

# ROSC
* If ROSC, get EKG, labs, and arrange for ICU care
* Consider doing neuro exam and starting cooling
